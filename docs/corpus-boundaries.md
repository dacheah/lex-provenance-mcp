# Corpus boundaries

What each corpus is, and — as importantly — where its edges are. The connector reflects the
canonical corpora's own scope decisions; it does not re-draw them.

## `space_law`
International and national space law: the UN space treaties, UN GA principle sets, COPUOS soft-law
guidelines, and selected national statutes (US, Luxembourg, France). **Not** general aviation law,
telecommunications/spectrum regulation, or export-control regimes.

## `deep_seabed_mining`
Law of mineral resources of "the Area": UNCLOS Part XI, the 1994 Implementation Agreement, ISA
exploration regulations and the draft Exploitation Regulations ("Mining Code"), the 2011 ITLOS
Advisory Opinion, sponsoring-state laws, and the US non-UNCLOS track (DSHMRA/OCSLA). **Not** general
law of the sea, fisheries, or environmental law unrelated to the Area. The US track is marked as a
distinct, non-UNCLOS alternative.

## `bbnj_high_seas`
The 2023 BBNJ Agreement ("High Seas Treaty") and its UNCLOS framework, its founding UN resolutions,
and Preparatory Commission / COP outputs. **Not** the deep-seabed-mining regime (a *separate* corpus —
cross-referenced, never merged), general fisheries management, or coastal-state jurisdiction.

## Cross-corpus relationships

The three corpora share a "global-commons / areas beyond national jurisdiction" thesis, and there are
genuine legal cross-references (e.g. BBNJ ↔ UNCLOS). **Resolving and reasoning over those
cross-references is not part of this open connector** — see [`commercial.md`](commercial.md). The open
tier keeps each corpus addressable on its own; it does not relate them for you.

## Sample vs full

This repo ships **sample manifests** (a few provisions per corpus). Coverage counts in each
`source_pointer.json` state the full-corpus size. Always treat the connector's local answers as a
demonstration slice; the canonical repos and datasets are authoritative.
